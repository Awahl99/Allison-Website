Fav Brand

Favorite Brand I was looking at all my personal hygiene products on my dresser. Most of my products have clean ingredients and simple logos. The one product that kept catching my attention was Frank Body. Looking at the product I had gave me design inspiration plus it is also one of my favorite beauty products. Frank Body is a skincare industry that has a fun twist to it. This brand sells anything from body, face, and hair products. Every single product they sell is vegan, cruelty free and Australia made. These features are why it is one of my favorite brands. Another reason why I love this brand is because they quote “don’t take themselves too seriously.” You will find yourself reading about how their products can help you feel better in your own skin. I didn’t want to get off their website not just because I was getting complements with every product, I was reading but because of their mesmerizing front choice.

Why Frank Body? There primarily font resembles typewritten text that takes you back to simpler times and transforms you to focus on the natural products. There brand name may look normal and ordinary but why did I spend ten minutes looking at the Frank Body section in the store? Because I was indeed transformed into the simpler times. The website has the same effect on you. The Frank Body website uses the typewritten font for all their product descriptions, header, and home page banner. Having this typography helps the customer focus more on the natural product itself. The primary colors are pink or white background with black text. Any color variations come through colored boxes describing the product, professional photos of the products or models demonstrating the products, and customer review photos. The products on the website have vibrant colors and a whitewashed background so the product itself can stand out to the customer.

What do I plan to use/ My logo banner I am a fan of the pop of color combination that Frank Body uses, I am also a fan of the typewritten font they use. The font type used for the header and body is antialiased. They smooth the font on the level of the pixel, as opposed to the subpixel. I plan to use a more of a san serif typograph for my logo header. I want more of a flow with the letters then a smooth font. I will be using the pop of color on a neutral background like Frank Body website. A pop of color brings a warm feeling to a logo.
